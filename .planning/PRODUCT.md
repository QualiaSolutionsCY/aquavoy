# PRODUCT — Aquavoy

> The "who and why" every road agent reads before designing or building.

## Register

**product** — design SERVES the product. This is an internal operations tool; the UI's job is to get out of the way of the conversation and the file/mail work, not to sell anything.

## Three Users

1. **Wency** — runs day-to-day back office for the shipping operation. Lives in the chat: "find the latest invoice for X in Verzonden Facturen and email it to the client." Wants the agent to just do it, with a confirm before anything sends.
2. **Jeanette** — handles correspondence and crew/recipient email prep. Uses the Prep page to draft 1:1 emails and the Emails page to manage scheduled sends. Wants drafts that sound like her, not like a bot.
3. **The owner (Wence)** — occasional check-ins; cares that nothing embarrassing or wrong gets sent on the company's behalf, and that files don't get deleted by accident.

## Brand Voice

**Calm · precise · maritime-operational.** The agent talks like a competent dispatcher, not a chatbot. It confirms before it acts, states what it did in plain terms, and never pads.

- *Error:* "Couldn't reach the mailbox — the IMAP login was refused. Want me to re-check the account credentials?"
- *Confirmation:* "Scheduled. The invoice email to client@example.com goes out tomorrow 09:00 Amsterdam time. Say the word to cancel."
- *Empty state:* "No connected OneDrive yet. Connect an account and I'll start finding files for you."

## Anti-References

1. **Generic SaaS chatbot widgets (Intercom-style bubbles)** — this is an operations console, not a support widget; it must feel like a tool the team lives in, not a popup.
2. **Consumer AI chat (ChatGPT's blank-canvas friendliness)** — too playful and unmoored; Aquavoy is domain-anchored (mailboxes, drive folders, crew) and should always show that context.
3. **Enterprise admin dashboards (heavy tables, dense toolbars, gray-on-gray)** — Aquavoy is two people, not an IT department; no permission matrices, no clutter, no corporate gray.

## One-Sentence Differentiation

The thing you remember 24 hours later: *you ran the company's files and email by talking to one calm agent that always asked before it sent.*
